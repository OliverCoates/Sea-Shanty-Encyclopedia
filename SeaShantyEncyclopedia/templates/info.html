What are shanties?

Sea shanties are traditionaly work songs that were sung onboard of merchant sailing vessels. Lately this term has expanded to encompass sea ballads and maritime folk songs. Sea shanties were used to organize labor on board of ships and co-ordinate pulls and pushes both on deck and with pumps

Sea shanties live on today with a small community of listeners. A few Sea shanty bands such as 'The Longest Johns' continue to record sea shanties and even make their own for the niche community.

"To me!" is a common phrase in found in nearly all traditional sea shanties. The "To Me!" was the call from the shantyman (The man leading the shanty) for the rest of the singers to sing out the refrain

Sea shanties rose to a small ammount of popularity through ubisofts release of 'Assasins creed: Black flag' which featured many sea shanties. Recent movies such as: "Blow the man down" and "Fisherman's freinds" have highlighted the shanty/folk singing community"

Sea Shanties fell out of fasion with the end of sailing ships, it was in the 1960's when sea shanties would experience a small revival with the American second folk revival. Sea shanties would continue to hold a marginal audience.
